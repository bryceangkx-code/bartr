## Brand Observations

### Strengths
- Clear, simple value proposition with zero ambiguity
- Strong geographic positioning (SEA/Singapore is a specific, underserved niche)
- "No minimum follower requirement" is a powerful differentiator vs. agency-based models
- No cash = removes the complexity of payment processing, appeals to nano/micro creators
- Blog content is well-targeted to SEO keywords in the creator economy space

### Gaps
- No custom typography — system fonts feel generic vs. competitors
- No social proof numbers yet (creator count, brand count, deals completed)
- No About page or founding story
- No pricing/credits page visible publicly
- Violet color palette is solid but could be more distinctive with a secondary accent
- Hero has no visual — just text and CTAs (a strong image/illustration would increase conversion)

### Tone Analysis
The copy is direct and benefit-focused. Uses second-person ("your content", "you"). Avoids buzzwords. Has a startup-authentic feel rather than enterprise-polished. Slightly understated — could lean harder into the SEA community angle.
